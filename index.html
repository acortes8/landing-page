There are over 20,000 known species of bees in seven recognized biological families. Learn everything there is to know about honey bees, bumble bees, mason bees, and more.

Some random information.

Honey bees are social bees that are known for their populous hives that can exceed numbers of 60,000. They are native only to mainland Afro-Eurasia and were imported to the Americas long ago. They construct perennial colonies using wax they secrete, and produce a surplus of honey which they store in great quantity.
Bumble bees are large social bees that live in small colonies of around 50 individuals. They use their large size to create resonant vibrations, making them capable of buzz pollination. They construct their colonies underground usually, utilizing abandoned rodent burrows and similar settings.
Mason bees are small solitary bees that do not create colonies. They nest and forage only for themselves, and they cannot sting as they do not have stingers. They construct nests for themselves using mud and sand.
Leafcutter bees are small solitary bees that also do not create colonies. They share many characteristics and behaviors with mason bees. They have scissor-like jaws which they use to cut apart leaves and petals. They use these pieces to line their nests.
